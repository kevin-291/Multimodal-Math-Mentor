TOPIC: Conic Sections (Parabola, Ellipse, Hyperbola)

FORMULAS & IDENTITIES:
- Parabola (y^2 = 4ax): Focus (a,0), Directrix x = -a. Parametric: (at^2, 2at).
- Ellipse (x^2/a^2 + y^2/b^2 = 1, a>b): Eccentricity e = √(1 - b^2/a^2). Foci: (±ae, 0). Parametric: (a cos θ, b sin θ).
- Hyperbola (x^2/a^2 - y^2/b^2 = 1): Eccentricity e = √(1 + b^2/a^2). Foci: (±ae, 0). Parametric: (a sec θ, b tan θ).
- Condition of tangency (y = mx + c):
  - Parabola: c = a/m
  - Ellipse: c^2 = a^2 m^2 + b^2
  - Hyperbola: c^2 = a^2 m^2 - b^2

SOLUTION TEMPLATE - Common Tangent to Two Conics:
Problem: Find the common tangent to y^2 = 4ax and x^2/a^2 + y^2/b^2 = 1.
Step 1: Write the general equation of a tangent to the first conic in terms of slope 'm'. (e.g., y = mx + a/m).
Step 2: Apply the condition of tangency for this line on the second conic.
Step 3: Substitute c = a/m into the second conic's tangency condition: (a/m)^2 = a^2 m^2 + b^2.
Step 4: Solve the resulting equation for 'm'.
Step 5: Substitute 'm' back into the tangent equation.

COMMON MISTAKES & PITFALLS:
- Confusing the ellipse eccentricity formula with the hyperbola one (sign error).
- Not checking whether 'a' > 'b' or 'b' > 'a' in an ellipse, which flips the major axis from x to y.
- Using the wrong parametric coordinates for the conjugate hyperbola.

UNIT/DOMAIN CONSTRAINTS:
- Eccentricity domains: Parabola (e = 1), Ellipse (0 < e < 1), Hyperbola (e > 1), Circle (e = 0).
- The value inside the square root for eccentricity must always be positive.
